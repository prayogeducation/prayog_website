2014

Location
Haliya Village, Nalgonda District, Hyderabad, India

Dates
1st to 22nd August 2014

Issue: Education

With over 287 million adults who cannot read or write, India has the highest number of illiterate adults. Using ASER figures, we estimate that over 100 million children in India are two or more years below their grade level. Under the current circumstances, such children are very unlikely to reach the levels of capability expected of children after eight years of schooling, as mandated by the Right to Education (RTE) Act. A severe lack of infrastructure, high pupil to teacher ratios and poor levels of teacher training all limit the possibilities for the vast majority of children and thereby, this perpetuates the cycle of poverty that currently grips the nation. Thus, the crucial need to consistently deliver world-class educational material to these children in order to educate them and provide them with technical skills required to become players in todayâ€™s global economy arises. Moreover, the benefits of well-proven, cost-free, online educational opportunities that are being presented through organisations such as Khan Academy and Coursera are only limited to those with access to computers and internet.

2015

Location
Haliya Village, Nalgonda District, Hyderabad, India

Dates
July(???)

Issue: Education

Having set up a learning centre and completed research demonstrating the benefits of using onlond-educational videos in conjuction with classroom teaching we want to go back to Hilaya Village to set up a permanent offline learning centre and train the teachers how to use it and make the most of the resources provided. This will then have the potential to be opened up for use by the whole community and will be low cost to expand. It will also provide an invaluable base for research into technology assisted education. We would like the learning centre to be sustainable so are curretnly looking into using solar power to charge the equiptment which consists of Raspberry Pis and tablets. Last year we found that an increased variety of matierial could be more helpful so we have also partnered with other media providers to diversify the video resources available to the teachers.
